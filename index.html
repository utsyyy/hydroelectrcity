Hydroelectricity


(Built by students of class 8C for science exhibition)

Hydroelectricity, or hydroelectric power, is electricity generated by harnessing the energy from flowing water. It is one of the most widely used forms of renewable energy and plays a significant role in the world's energy mix.

Hydroelectric power plants convert the kinetic energy of flowing or falling water into mechanical energy, which is then used to generate electricity. The basic components of a hydroelectric power plant include a dam, reservoir, turbines, and generators.

  • Renewable and sustainable source of energy
  • Low greenhouse gas emissions
  • Reliable and consistent power generation
  • Can provide flood control and water supply
  • Potential environmental impact on aquatic ecosystems
  • High initial costs for construction
  • Dependency on water availability
